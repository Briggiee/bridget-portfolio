Wide World Importers Business Intelligence & Advanced Analytics

Business Intelligence Case Study

Executive Summary

This project demonstrates the development of an end-to-end Business Intelligence and Advanced Analytics solution for Wide World Importers using SQL, Power BI, Power Query, DAX and Excel. The solution consolidates operational and commercial data into a centralized reporting environment that enables management to monitor revenue performance, customer activity, supplier contribution, supply chain trends, profitability, warehouse operations, financial reporting and forecasting from a single analytics platform.

Business Problem

Wide World Importers operates across multiple business functions including customer sales, supplier purchases, inventory movement, invoicing, warehouse operations and financial transactions. Without a centralized analytical framework, it becomes difficult for management to monitor overall business performance, identify revenue drivers, understand cost pressure, track supplier dependency, evaluate customer behavior and plan effectively for future demand.

The business required a comprehensive Business Intelligence solution that would unify these operational data points into a structured reporting model and provide actionable insights for executive decision-making.

Project Objectives

  • Develop a centralized executive dashboard for monitoring commercial and operational performance.
  • Track sales performance, order volume and revenue growth across time.
  • Analyze customer activity, customer contribution and buying behavior.
  • Evaluate supplier performance, procurement concentration and supply chain activity.
  • Assess profitability, cost pressure and margin performance.
  • Monitor financial reporting metrics including payments and outstanding balances.
  • Review warehouse activity, stock movement and operational distribution.
  • Support planning through time intelligence analysis and forecasting.
  • Generate strategic insights and recommendations for business improvement.

Data Source & Technical Workflow

This project was built using the Wide World Importers sample database backup sourced from Kaggle. The solution followed an end-to-end analytics workflow beginning with database setup in SQL and ending with a multi-page reporting solution in Power BI.

🗄 SQL Database Setup

Downloaded the Wide World Importers backup file from Kaggle and loaded the database into a SQL environment to serve as the primary data source for the project.

🔗 Power BI Connection

Connected Power BI directly to the SQL database and imported only the relevant tables required for analysis.

🧹 Data Cleaning & Transformation

Removed unnecessary tables, dropped irrelevant columns, transformed fields and prepared the data for reporting using Power Query Editor.

🔄 Data Modeling & DAX

Built relationships across the selected business tables and created DAX measures for revenue, profitability, customer metrics, supplier performance, warehouse analysis, financial reporting and forecasting.

Key Business Insights

The dashboard analysis uncovered several commercial, operational and financial insights across Wide World Importers’ business activities.

💰 Revenue Growth with Profitability Pressure

The business generated strong revenue and order volume, but profitability remained under pressure, suggesting that high operational or procurement-related costs were eroding margins.

👥 Customer Concentration

A relatively small group of customers contributed a significant share of total revenue, highlighting concentration opportunities and risks.

🧾 Supplier Dependency

Procurement activity was concentrated among a few suppliers, indicating the need to monitor supplier dependency and vendor risk more closely.

📉 Profitability Gaps

Some categories and business areas generated revenue but delivered weak profit outcomes, indicating a need for cost and pricing review.

🏬 Warehouse Visibility

Warehouse and inventory reporting showed uneven movement across products and locations, suggesting opportunities to improve stock planning and distribution efficiency.

🔮 Forecast Stability

Forecasting suggested relatively stable demand patterns, supporting the use of forward-looking planning for sales and inventory decisions.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Review cost structure and margin performance across low-profit product categories to improve profitability.
  • Monitor top-customer concentration and develop broader customer acquisition strategies to reduce dependency risk.
  • Evaluate supplier concentration and negotiate better procurement terms with dominant vendors.
  • Use time-based analysis and forecasting outputs to improve demand planning and commercial planning.
  • Improve warehouse visibility and stock allocation to reduce inefficiencies in product distribution and inventory movement.
  • Incorporate dashboard findings into executive review meetings for faster, evidence-based decision-making.
