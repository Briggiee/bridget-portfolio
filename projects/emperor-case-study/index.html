Emperor Logistics & Supply Chain Performance Dashboard

Business Intelligence Case Study

Executive Summary

This project demonstrates the design and development of an end-to-end Supply Chain and Logistics Performance Dashboard for Emperor Logistics using Power BI. The solution consolidates operational data across orders, deliveries, procurement, inventory, fleet operations, finance, customer service, and returns into a single reporting environment that enables management to monitor business performance, identify operational bottlenecks, control logistics costs, and make faster data-driven decisions.

Business Problem

Logistics and supply chain businesses often manage high volumes of operational data across multiple functions such as procurement, warehousing, transport, customer service, and financial operations. When this information is spread across disconnected reports and spreadsheets, it becomes difficult for management to maintain visibility into business performance, monitor service quality, identify cost drivers, and respond quickly to operational issues.

Emperor Logistics required a centralized Business Intelligence solution that would provide a unified view of logistics performance across the supply chain, support executive reporting, and help management track the key drivers of service delivery, cost efficiency, customer returns, and operational effectiveness.

Project Objectives

  • Develop an executive dashboard for logistics and supply chain operations.
  • Monitor order fulfillment and delivery performance.
  • Track procurement spending, supplier activity, and purchase trends.
  • Analyze inventory levels and warehouse distribution.
  • Evaluate fleet utilization, shipment activity, and transport performance.
  • Assess financial cost drivers such as fuel, maintenance, and operating cost.
  • Monitor customer service performance, return volume, and return reasons.
  • Generate strategic recommendations to improve operational efficiency and service quality.

Dataset Overview

The dashboard was developed using a logistics and supply chain dataset containing transactional, operational, inventory, procurement, fleet, and customer return data. Multiple related tables were modeled in Power BI to provide a comprehensive view of Emperor Logistics’ business operations.

Dataset Description
Orders Customer orders, order dates, order value and fulfillment-related information.
Deliveries Delivery records, fulfillment status, delivery completion and shipment execution.
Purchases Procurement transactions, purchase cost, category-level spend and supplier purchases.
Suppliers Supplier information used to analyze procurement activity and supplier contribution.
Inventory Product stock levels, warehouse allocation and inventory distribution across categories.
Warehouses Warehouse records used to evaluate stock distribution and storage coverage.
Vehicles Vehicle information for tracking fleet size, vehicle type and transport operations.
Drivers Driver records supporting shipment and fleet performance analysis.
Shipments Shipment transactions, shipment status, vehicle usage and transport activity.
Fuel Logs Fuel spending and transport cost records used in operational cost analysis.
Maintenance Maintenance cost data for evaluating fleet servicing and upkeep expenditure.
Returns Returned deliveries, return reasons, return cost and customer service issues.
Customers Customer records supporting order analysis, service trends and return monitoring.

Dashboard Design

The dashboard was designed as a multi-page reporting solution that allows management to move from a high-level executive overview into more detailed analysis across each major logistics function. The report structure follows a logical business flow, beginning with overall operational performance and drilling into fulfillment, procurement, inventory, fleet, finance, and customer service.

📊 Executive Overview

Presents a consolidated summary of orders, deliveries, purchase cost, active vehicles and customer satisfaction.

🚚 Operational Dashboards

Dedicated pages were created for Orders & Deliveries, Procurement, Inventory, Fleet, Finance, and Customer Service analysis.

🎯 User Experience

Clean navigation, consistent formatting, KPI cards and intuitive visuals were used to improve readability and decision-making.

⚡ Performance Optimization

DAX measures and a structured data model were used to ensure efficient filtering and responsive report performance.

Key Business Insights

The analysis uncovered several operational and financial insights across logistics execution, procurement, inventory, fleet performance and customer service.

📦 Order Fulfillment

Order and delivery volume remained strong across the reporting period, but fulfillment performance showed opportunities to improve completed delivery execution.

🧾 Procurement Spend

Procurement spending represented a major cost driver, with some suppliers and categories accounting for a significant share of total purchase cost.

🏬 Inventory Visibility

Inventory levels were high across multiple warehouses, highlighting the need for better stock allocation and inventory planning.

🚛 Fleet Costs

Fuel and maintenance costs represented a significant share of logistics operating expenses, making fleet cost control a key management priority.

📞 Customer Returns

Return analysis revealed that late deliveries and order issues were major drivers of customer dissatisfaction and return-related cost.

💰 Financial Efficiency

Total logistics cost was heavily influenced by procurement, fuel and maintenance, reinforcing the need for ongoing cost monitoring and operational optimization.

Strategic Recommendations

Based on the insights obtained from the dashboard, the following actions are recommended to improve logistics efficiency, cost control and customer satisfaction.

  • Improve delivery execution by investigating incomplete or delayed deliveries and resolving last-mile fulfillment bottlenecks.
  • Negotiate better terms with top suppliers and monitor high-spend categories to improve procurement efficiency.
  • Optimize warehouse allocation and stock planning to reduce overstock risk and improve inventory utilization.
  • Reduce fleet operating cost through better fuel monitoring, route optimization and proactive maintenance planning.
  • Track customer return reasons closely and address recurring issues such as late delivery, wrong item dispatch and damaged items.
  • Use the dashboard during operational and executive review meetings to support faster, evidence-based decision-making.
